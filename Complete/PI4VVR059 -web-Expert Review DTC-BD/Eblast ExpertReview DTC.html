Expert Review in Relapsed/Refractory Differentiated Thyroid Cancer

This CME-certified Expert Review features discussions on relapsed/refractory differentiated thyroid cancer (DTC).

Discussion Topics

  • Incidence, histologic classification, and prognosis for thyroid cancer
  • Clinical presentation of a patient with thyroid cancer
  • Prognostic factors and the methods for making the diagnosis of DTC
  • Role of molecular testing and the molecular targets known in DTC
  • Initial treatment of DTC
  • Definition, prognosis, and asymptomatic characteristics of patients with radioactive iodine refractory (RAI-R) DTC
  • Treatment of RAI-R DTC
  • Ongoing clinical trial for RAI-R DTC

Target Audience

This activity is intended to meet the needs of practicing medical oncologists and other healthcare professionals involved in the treatment of patients with DTC.

» View Activity

___________________________________________________________

Learning Objectives

After successful completion of this educational activity, participants should be able to:

  • Describe the patients’ clinical presentation, key prognostic factors, and methods for making the diagnosis of DTC
  • Apply recent advances to the treatment of patients with RAI-R DTC, including integration of tyrosine kinase inhibitors
  • Describe the role of molecular testing and the molecular targets known in DTC
  • Identify appropriate strategies for evaluating patients with relapsed/refractory differentiated thyroid cancer
